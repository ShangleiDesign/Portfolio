ArcGIS Hub Sites Design Process

After many project collaborations with various teams, stakeholders and clients, I reviewed and analyzed the common practice patterns and formalized the ArcGIS Hub Sites design process, which now becomes project scoping standard to guide any ArcGIS Hub Sites relevant design and implementation projects.

Kick-off Stage

  • Initial questionaries and comminutions
  • Confirm timeline and milestones
  • Understand requirements, exportations and goals
  • Share examples, references, and resources

Design Stage

  • Includes wireframe, low-fidelity and high-fidelity mockups, and 2-3 design reviews.
  • All design elements/contents are to be confirmed at this stage, such as texts, logos, icons, images, links apps/maps, etc.

Build Stage

  • Implement the Hub sites and pages, and make it functional as expected.
  • Includes customized html/css codes and graphic assets

Final Review Stage

  • Includes last round of site review and design refining if needed.
  • Client should sign-off the project at this stage

Tech Transfer Stage (Optional)

  • Tech transfer workshop will be provided if needed.

Showcase Projects

Census Covid-19 Dataset Hub

Census Covid-19 Dataset Hub is a resource page on census.gov to help federal agencies, businesses and communities make decisions related to the COVID-19 pandemic. Similar to the Census Bureau’s resource pages created during natural disasters, this resource page includes information on population demographics, economic indicators and businesses. It features a new interactive data hub that centralizes data previously released from the American Community Survey and the County Business Patterns program to facilitate users’ access to data useful in pandemic-related decision-making.

HCD CA Statewide Housing Plan Hub Site

California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD) launched their digital Statewide Housing Plan (SHP). HCD has released housing plans in the past as PDF documents that have been several hundred pages long, time consuming to produce, and relevant only to a small audience for a short period of time. Recognizing the importance of housing in California and data as the foundation for policy decisions, HCD requested Esri support in transforming the 2022 SHP to be delivered through a digital format.

The SHP includes:

  1. An ArcGIS Hub site that serves as the executive summary and provides access to a collection of housing data resources.
  2. A StoryMap that is the complete policy document of the Statewide Housing Plan (accessible from the Hub site)
  3. A collection of ArcGIS Dashboards, Experiences, and maps that are used in the StoryMap and on the Hub site

This effort was a huge organizational shift in the way the team at HCD approaches policy documents.

By providing convenient access to a broad range of information the HUD-eGIS Storefront empowers citizens and organizations to engage in the data-driven decision making efforts that affect their communities.
